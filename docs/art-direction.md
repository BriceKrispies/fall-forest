# Art Direction

## Visual laws

1. **No empty ground near the camera.** Foreground must always have grass clumps, flowers, shrubs, stumps, logs, or rocks. Bare terrain is not acceptable close to the viewer.

2. **No long sightlines.** The forest is enclosed by clustered tree masses, bushes, and fog. The player cannot see far ahead clearly.

3. **Every few meters should feel authored.** The path is a sequence of small scenic moments — flower patches, denser groves, log crossings, rock clusters, tighter corridors, small clearings.

4. **Shapes must be chunky and readable.** Bold silhouettes, fat canopy masses, simple props, exaggerated forms. No thin noisy detail or fragile sign-like trees.

5. **Slight scale exaggeration is encouraged.** Oversized flowers, chunky bushes, slightly-too-narrow path — diorama charm over realism.

## Shape language

- **Trees**: Layered cones/pyramids with 5-7 sides, slightly asymmetric, short thick trunks. Three distinct archetypes (tall symmetric, offset chunky, narrow spire) with variation via scale and rotation.
- **Bushes**: Stacked low pyramids, compact and rounded in silhouette.
- **Rocks**: Irregular pyramids, grouped in clusters of 2-3.
- **Stumps**: Short wide cylinders with a slightly wider cap.
- **Logs**: Pentagonal cross-section tubes lying on ground.
- **Flowers**: Tiny pyramids on thin stems, clustered in patches.
- **Grass**: Simple blade triangles in small clumps.

## Palette direction

| Element | Color range |
|---------|-------------|
| Path | Warm brown/tan (0.55, 0.4, 0.25) |
| Ground | Mossy dark green (0.2–0.3 green channel) |
| Pine foliage | Dark → mid → light green, layered per tree |
| Trunks | Dark warm brown |
| Flowers | Cream, pink, gold, muted red — localized accents |
| Rocks | Cool grey, slightly warm |
| Fog/sky | Warm haze (0.78, 0.75, 0.62) |
| Sunlit faces | Warm golden tint |
| Shaded faces | Cool blue-grey tint |

## Scene composition rules

- Path winds through the scene, never straight for more than a few meters
- Trees are layered: close inner ring (2-3m from path), middle ring (5-6m), far wall (7-8m)
- Bushes fill gaps between trees and hug the path edges
- Ground cover (grass, flowers) fills every open patch near the camera
- Logs and stumps act as anchor points at intervals along the path
- Shadow patches under major props give grounding
- Fog limits visibility to ~35m, creating natural enclosure

## What is off-style

- Generic flat-triangle-on-pole trees
- Large empty flat ground areas
- Sparse placement with regular spacing
- Realistic proportions or scale
- Bright saturated primary colors
- Long straight paths with open views
- Procedurally random placement without compositional intent
- Thin wireframe-like geometry
- Any UI, HUD, or debug overlays in the final piece
